# Product

## Register
product

## Users
Job seekers, professionals, and students preparing for job interviews. They use this tool in a focused, private environment — a home office, bedroom, or quiet study space — with access to a webcam and microphone. They are self-motivated but may be anxious about their performance. They come to the tool seeking honest, objective feedback that helps them improve, not just feel good.

## Product Purpose
To provide objective, multimodal AI-powered feedback on interview answers — analyzing speech cadence, facial emotion, and semantic relevance simultaneously — so users can practice, identify concrete weaknesses, and build genuine confidence before real interviews. Success means a user finishing a session with specific, actionable next steps, not just a score.

## Brand Personality
Professional. Precise. Encouraging.

The product feels like a trusted technical advisor, not a cheerleader or a judge. It is authoritative without being cold. It gives users the sense they are interacting with genuinely advanced technology, not a consumer app dressed up to look smart.

Three words: **Expert. Objective. Empowering.**

## Anti-references
- Do NOT look like a sterile academic testing tool or research paper interface (no plain white backgrounds, no dense tabular layouts without context).
- Do NOT look like a generic SaaS dashboard (no sidebar nav with 12 items, no gray-on-gray card grids, no plain progress bars without data meaning).
- Do NOT look like a playful social app (no bubbly fonts, no celebration confetti, no gamification reward loops).
- Do NOT look like an enterprise HR tool (no stiff corporate color palettes, no bureaucratic form-heaviness).

## Design Principles
- **Show, don't tell.** Visualize every metric rather than listing numbers. An animated ring communicates confidence better than "Confidence: High" in a table row.
- **Expert confidence.** The interface should feel stable, technologically advanced, and precise. Every data point is presented with professional intent — never decorative.
- **Encouraging objectivity.** Feedback reads like a knowledgeable coach, never a harsh critic. Weaknesses are framed as growth vectors.
- **Connected spaces.** Every screen — Landing, Analyze, Results — should feel like a different workspace inside the same AI operating system. Navigation between them is seamless, not jarring.
- **The data is the design.** Processing animations, metric bars, and score rings are not decorative chrome — they ARE the product. They carry meaning and must be legible and trustworthy at all times.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
- WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Body text must maintain ≥4.5:1 contrast against dark backgrounds.
- Do not rely on color alone to communicate state (confidence, score grade, emotion). Pair color with label text, icon, or shape.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`: all entrance animations and scanning border loops must have reduced-motion alternatives (crossfade or instant transition).
- Support graceful degradation when a user's camera is low quality or unavailable — the emotion analysis pipeline must surface a clear "no face detected" state rather than silently failing.
- Touch targets ≥ 44×44px on all interactive elements.
